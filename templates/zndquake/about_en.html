About ZundaQuake

It will deliver earthquake information to you faster and more clearly.

⚠️ About using the beta version

The version currently available is under development. Not all features listed on this page are available for use.

Feature Introduction

P-wave and S-wave arrival prediction

Based on the Japan Meteorological Agency's Earthquake Early Warnings, it displays a real-time countdown of the predicted arrival times for the P-wave (initial tremors) and S-wave (main tremors) at your current location.

Detailed seismic intensity map

Displays not only the epicentre but also the predicted seismic intensity for each region colour-coded on a map. This allows you to intuitively grasp the spread of shaking.

Disaster Information

Displays and alerts you to not only earthquakes and tsunamis, but also volcanic eruption information and localised weather warnings. This helps raise your awareness of disaster prevention on a daily basis.

Overseas Earthquake Information

Receives earthquake information not only from Japan, but also from South Korea, the Republic of China (Taiwan), Chile, and Mexico. You can quickly obtain earthquake information from around the world.

Voice Read-Aloud Function

Notifies you of earthquake occurrence information via synthetic voice. You can obtain information even when you cannot see the screen.

History Display

Review the history of past earthquakes. Detailed data allows for retrospective analysis.

About this software

Origin of the name

The name derives from "zunda" (from the local dish zunda mochi) and "quake" (meaning earthquake), taken from the Tohoku Region Pacific Coast Earthquake(Great East Japan Earthquake) that struck the Tohoku region on 11 March 2011(JST). It was named with the intention of not forgetting the lessons of the Great East Japan Earthquake and raising awareness of disaster prevention.

Development background

The beta release date of 11 March 2026 marks the 15th anniversary of the Great East Japan Earthquake. Developed to prevent such tragedies from recurring, ZundaQuake aims to be both high-performance and lightweight, while remaining simple for anyone to use.
